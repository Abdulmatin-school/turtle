Why Turtles Are Endangered

Many turtle species are endangered due to human activities. Pollution, plastic waste, habitat loss, and climate change have made it harder for turtles to survive. Fishing nets and plastic bags are especially dangerous because turtles can mistake them for food.

Major Threats to Turtles

  • Plastic pollution – turtles may eat plastic or get trapped in it
  • Habitat destruction – beaches and wetlands are disappearing
  • Climate change – warmer sand affects turtle hatchlings
  • Fishing practices – turtles can be caught accidentally in nets

How We Can Help Save Turtles

Humans play a big role in protecting turtles. Small actions can make a big difference when many people work together.

  • Reduce plastic use and recycle whenever possible
  • Never leave trash on beaches or near water
  • Support organizations that protect wildlife
  • Respect nesting areas and wildlife rules

Why Saving Turtles Matters

Turtles help keep ecosystems healthy. Protecting turtles also protects oceans, rivers, and many other animals that share their environment.
